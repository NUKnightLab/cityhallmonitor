Glossary

City Hall Monitor gives you access to a plethora of documents. We decided to group the different types of documents to help users find what they are looking for. Here is a glossary that will define what the groups are and show you some example documents that belong to each.

Claims

People and organizations getting refunds from the government. A lot of "condominium claims", sewer and refuse refunds. Also, some payments from the city to firefighters and policemen.

Proceedings

Traffic

Documents establishing tow zones, buffer zones (for parking), traffic signs and signals, vehicle weight limits, traffic direction of a street, parking prohibited, etc.

Parking

Various types of parking permits (handicapped, residential, industrial). Also includes amendments to the municipal code on laws related to parking.

Recognition

Exemptions

Public Use

These are mostly the City of Chicago granting various organizations the right to use public land/property. For example, allowing Corner Bakery to set up sidewalk cafes or have awnings or canopies. Includes "grants of privilege in public way" and "issuance of permits for warrants" to use public areas.

Tax

Permits

Tag day permits, special licenses and permits

Licenses

Developments

Services & Budgets

Interesting Documents

Documents that stood out to us. While the type of document may be similar to those in other categories, the interesting thing about these is their subject matter. Includes documents such as letters of support for reparations to victims of torture by chicago police department, special city council meetings, calls for hearings and support for various non-standard things. Also includes settlement agreements, expenditure of open space impact fee funds for development projects, expenditure of various taxes.
